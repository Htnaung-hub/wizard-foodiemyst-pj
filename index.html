Let's explore the Vietnamese food!

Vietnamese cuisine is celebrated for its fresh ingredients, vibrant flavors, and healthy cooking techniques. It emphasizes a balance of sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and spicy tastes , often incorporating fresh herbs, vegetables, and lean proteins. Rice and noodles are staples, alongside a variety of seafood, meats, and regional specialties.

Phở

- A fragrant noodle soup with beef or chicken, herbs, and spices.

Phở

  • Ingredients:
    • Rice noodles
    • Beef or chicken
    • Beef bones
    • Star anise, cloves, cinnamon
    • Fish sauce, onion, ginger
    • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil)
  • Recipe:
    • Simmer beef bones with spices for hours to create broth.
    • Cook noodles separately.
    • Add meat slices, herbs, and serve with lime.

Bánh Chưng

- Square sticky rice cake filled with mung beans and pork, traditionally made for Tết (Lunar New Year).

Bánh Chưng

  • Ingredients:
    • Glutinous rice
    • Mung beans
    • Pork belly
    • Shallots, fish sauce, pepper
    • Banana leaves (for wrapping)
  • Recipe:
    • Soak rice and beans overnight.
    • Marinate pork with shallots and pepper.
    • Layer rice, beans, and pork, wrap in banana leaves.
    • Boil for 8–10 hours until fully cooked.

Gỏi Cuốn

- Fresh spring rolls made with rice paper, filled with shrimp, herbs, and vermicelli.

Gỏi Cuốn

  • Ingredients:
    • Rice paper
    • Shrimp
    • Pork
    • Vermicelli noodles
    • Lettuce, mint, basil
  • Recipe:
    • Soak rice paper briefly in water.
    • Layer shrimp, pork, vermicelli, and herbs.
    • Roll tightly and serve with peanut sauce.

Cá Kho Tộ

- Caramelized fish cooked in a clay pot, often served with rice.

Cá Kho Tộ

  • Ingredients:
    • Catfish or similar fish
    • Fish sauce
    • Caramel sauce (sugar + water)
    • Garlic, shallots
    • Chili, black pepper
  • Recipe:
    • Caramelize sugar in a clay pot.
    • Add fish, fish sauce, garlic, and chili.
    • Simmer until the sauce thickens.

Bánh Mì

- A Vietnamese sandwich with various fillings, typically including pork, vegetables, and herbs.

Bánh Mì

  • Ingredients:
    • Baguette
    • Grilled pork or cold cuts
    • Pâté, mayonnaise
    • Pickled carrots and daikon
    • Cucumber, cilantro, chili
  • Recipe:
    • Toast baguette lightly.
    • Spread pâté and mayonnaise.
    • Add meat, pickles, and vegetables.

Bún Bò Huế

- A spicy beef noodle soup from Huế, known for its rich broth and lemongrass flavor.

Bún Bò Huế

  • Ingredients:
    • Beef shank
    • Pork knuckles
    • Lemongrass
    • Shrimp paste, fish sauce
    • Rice noodles
    • Chili oil, fresh herbs
  • Recipe:
    • Simmer beef and pork bones with lemongrass.
    • Season broth with shrimp paste and fish sauce.
    • Serve with noodles and herbs.

Xôi

- Sticky rice that can be served sweet or savory, often topped with meats or mung beans.

Xôi

  • Ingredients:
    • Glutinous rice
    • Coconut milk (optional)
    • Mung beans
    • Peanuts
    • Fried shallots
    • Meats (for savory version)
  • Recipe:
    • Steam sticky rice until fully cooked.
    • Top with sweet or savory toppings.

Chè

- A sweet dessert soup made with beans, fruits, and coconut milk.

Chè

  • Ingredients:
    • Mung beans, red beans
    • Fruits, jelly
    • Coconut milk
    • Pandan leaves (optional)
    • Sugar
  • Recipe:
    • Boil beans with sugar until soft.
    • Add coconut milk and toppings like jelly or fruit.
